Mathematical Modeling

"If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."
A.Einstein

PROBLEM IDENTIFICATION

What is the problem?
Is it similar to something already known?
How to simplify it?
The better answer we find the closer to reality will be the model.

COLLISION WITH REALITY

Even best models are not able to fully describe the reality. That's why it's important to maintain consciousness of the difference between the ideal mathematcial world and chaos.

OUTCOMES

As soon as proper assumptions are made, we are able to start calculations. For that we mainly use frameworks like Wolfram Mathematica , MatLab, R or Python. Knowledge of those tools is essential in developing models.

COOPERATION

Usage of modern mathematical methods helps industry to evolve. That's why cooperation with experienced engineers is so important for us.

INTERDISCIPLINARITY

We are not scared by new challenges and treat each problem as equal. Mathematician has amazing ability of adapting his knowledge to any new project. It doesn't matter if it's going to be a simulation of computed tomography, data optimization or examining the nature of liquids.

SOLUTIONS

Solution doesn't close the problem, it is not a main purpose itself. Often looking for a solution we ask new questions, start to understand more and discover unknow aspects of studied phenomenon. Solution inspire our minds to find new conclusions, to change our perception and finally to develope better modeling methods.
