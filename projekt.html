Koło Naukowe Modelowania Matematycznego

Interdyscyplinarny zespół studentów z Politechniki Warszawskiej, który problemy traktuje jako kolejne zadanie do rozwiązania.

Kreatywni

Classical approach for preference modelling is based on one binary relation called weak preference. Let a and b to be a pair of alternatives from defined set A. Then R is called a weak preference relation when

\begin{center} aRb $\iff$ “a is not worse than b” \end{center}

Such definition of R implies that it has to be reflexive relation (aRa holds for any a $\in A$). Someone may indicate that in real life we use phrases like "a is better than b" or "no difference between a and b". Often it happens also that we are unable to compare two alternatives. The above R relation allows us to answer to above needs.

Let P, I, J be relations defined as follows:

aPb $\iff$ aRb and not bRa (a is better than b) (1.1) \\ aIb $\iff$ aRb and bRa (no difference between a and b) (1.2) \\ aJb $\iff$ not aRb and not bRa (a and b cannot be compared) (1.3) \\

The verbal expression could be simply represented by set-theoric operations as:

$P = R \cap R^d$ (1.4) \\ $I = R \cap R^{-1}$ (1.5) \\ $J = R^c \cap R^d$ (1.6) \\
